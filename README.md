Economic and Strategic Assessment of the Great Nicobar Project: Balancing Development, Sustainability and National Security

Project Overview

This project examines the Great Nicobar Development Project through the interconnected lenses of economic development, environmental sustainability, and national security. Situated at the crossroads of the Bay of Bengal and the Indo-Pacific region, Great Nicobar Island has emerged as a strategically significant location for India's maritime ambitions, regional connectivity initiatives, and long-term economic growth.

The study evaluates the proposed infrastructure components, including the Galathea Bay International Transshipment Port, Greenfield International Airport, power infrastructure, and township development. It further analyzes their potential impact on trade, logistics, tourism, maritime security, regional integration, and ecological conservation.

The project is inspired by insights gathered during a policy dialogue on the Andaman and Nicobar Islands involving policymakers, maritime experts, diplomats, military leaders, and development practitioners.

Objectives

- Assess the economic viability of the Great Nicobar Project.
- Analyze the strategic importance of Great Nicobar in the Indo-Pacific region.
- Examine the role of maritime infrastructure in strengthening India's supply chain resilience.
- Evaluate environmental and sustainability challenges associated with large-scale island development.
- Propose a balanced framework integrating development, sustainability, and national security.

Methodology

The project adopts a qualitative and policy-oriented research approach based on:

- Conference observations and expert discussions
- Government reports and policy documents
- Academic literature review
- Stakeholder Analysis
- Strategic Framework Assessment

Expected Outcomes

The study aims to develop a comprehensive understanding of how the Great Nicobar Project can contribute to India's economic growth and strategic objectives while ensuring environmental sustainability and responsible governance.
